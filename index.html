About

Who I am

I am a Masters student at Miami University in Oxford, OH studying Mechanical Engineering. I have just graduated with my undergraduate degrees – B.S. Mechanical Engineering and B.A. Music Performance – from Miami University.

I perform research studying applications of advanced materials with Dr. Jeong-Hoi Koo. Projects

01

Senior Capstone — Powell Industries Fastener Sorting System

Working with a team of 4 other students, I designed, built, and tested an automatic fastener-sorting system for Powell Industries. The system is built around a camera running a machine learning classification algorithm that identifies each fastener type. Once identified, a PLC, pneumatics, HMI, and conveyor work together to route parts to the correct bin. I was responsible for all system wiring and played a major role in the overall design process.

Our team won first place at the 2026 Miami University Senior Design Expo among all mechanical engineering projects.

02

MME 305 — Automatic Pancake Maker

Working with one other student, I wired and programmed an autonomous pancake-making work cell that guides a user through each step of cooking a pancake. After the first step, the system requires zero user input, using a load cell and thermocouple to confirm each step is complete before advancing.

The wiring stack included a DAQ, solid-state relay, instrumentation amplifier, low-pass filter, thermocouple amplifier, and linear voltage regulator. Every test user successfully made a pancake — mission accomplished.

03

MME 231 — Pump Project

Designed, manufactured, and tested a rotary pump alongside a team of 3 other students. Every component was hand-manufactured from stock aluminum using a variety of machining and manufacturing processes. The finished pump outperformed all other pumps in the lab section.

04

Semi-Automated Hydroponic System — TSA National Champions

With a team of 5 other students, I designed, built, tested, and presented a semi-automated hydroponic system enabling food banks to grow fresh vegetables in a compact footprint. The project was our entry for the Technology Student Association (TSA) 2022 Engineering Design competition, responding to the prompt “Restore or improve urban infrastructure.” The system was designed to combat urban food deserts while minimizing floor space within food banks.

The design went through multiple iterations and three testing phases, during which yield improved from 30% to 90%. We presented at the 2022 TSA National Conference and were selected as the national champions for Engineering Design.
